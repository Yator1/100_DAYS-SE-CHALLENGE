Prof. Wangari Maathai

The Woman of trees

Wangari Maathai delivers a speech 10 December 2004 at the Oslo City Hall after receiving her award from Nobel Committee President Ole Mjoes

Here is a brief timeline of Prof. Wangari's life:

  • 1960: Born on April 1st in Nyeri, Kenya
  • 1964: Earned a degree in Biological Sciences from Mount St. Scholastica College in Atchison, Kansas
  • 1966: Earned a Master of Science degree from the University of Pittsburgh
  • 1971: Became the first woman in East Africa to earn a Ph.D. from the University of Nairobi
  • 1976: Introduced the idea of planting trees with local communities through the National Council of Women of Kenya
  • 1984: Received the Right Livelihood Award
  • 2004: Received the Nobel Peace Prize
  • 2006: Received the Indira Gandhi Peace Prize
  • 2011: Passed away on September 25th at the age of 71
